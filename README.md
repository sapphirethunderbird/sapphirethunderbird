# 💫 About Me:
I moved to Japan from the US at 11 with zero Japanese, went through Japanese public middle and high school entirely in Japanese, and tested into a national university against native speakers. Getting fluent fast in systems that weren't built for me is how I learned to operate.
## What I Actually Do
- **Build AI tools from model to interface.** [Real-time burnout detection from facial expression](https://github.com/sapphirethunderbird/burnout_predictor) with MobileNetV2 trained on FER2013, OpenCV inference loop, PyTorch, local-first so the user owns their data.
- **Rebuild my own work as I get better at it, *in public*.** I reason out loud about what past-me got wrong, and I'm not afraid of being wrong. I'd rather rebuild the right thing than polish the wrong one.
- **Drive the call when the problem is wrong, not just the code.** On Voice-SCaNN I prototyped a proof-of-concept in under a week to pivot my team off the wrong problem. It earned an [official university press release](https://www.yamaguchi-u.ac.jp/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/25102701.pdf).

I care what I ship, and I think it's never the user's fault when something doesn't work. That's what the build needs to fix.

# Currently Building: Burnout Predictor
I'm rebuilding [burnout_predictor](https://github.com/sapphirethunderbird/burnout_predictor) from the ground up.

The first version had a major flaw hiding in plain sight: it loaded the model's default weights, not the ones I actually trained. I'm going to take this rebuild as an opportunity to be able to explain every decision made: the training loop, the inference pipeline, the local-first data model; not just the parts AI helped me write. 

[Watch the repo](https://github.com/sapphirethunderbird/burnout_predictor) to see it happen in real time.
